Context

Design in service of what matters

Una Gota Más is a social design campaign created for WaterAid — an international nonprofit founded in 1981, working in over 30 countries to deliver clean water, decent toilets and good hygiene to the people who need them most. The campaign's central premise: adding even one drop can change the whole.

Graphic design is used as a tool for awareness and collective mobilization. Black-and-white photography, bold typography and hard data combine to create pieces that demand attention. The palette strips back to water blue against monochrome emptiness — color as information, not decoration.

◉
The drop as symbol

A water drop is universally legible and loaded with meaning. In the campaign it works simultaneously as a sign of scarcity, of value, and of hope.

◎
Black & white + blue

B&W photography strips reality of the superfluous and forces the eye toward what's essential. Blue #29ABE2 cuts through as urgent data — not decoration.

◈
Adding up as action

"Una gota más" — one more drop. The campaign name is an invitation, not a plea. Design as both ethical and aesthetic imperative.

Campaign strategy

Three phases,
one goal

01
Awareness

Posters and digital pieces led by hard data. The goal is to stop the scroll and force the reader to sit with the number. Black, white and blue only.

02
Mobilization

Social media content with a specific call to action. Share, tag, commit. Every repost is one more drop added to the collective effort.

03
Concrete action

Institutional material for WaterAid showing how to get involved: donations, advocacy, supporting universal access to clean water, sanitation and hygiene.
