Banksy operates in the space between public and private, legal and illegal, visible and anonymous. A book about him must carry that same tension in its form.

The design challenge: honor the rawness of street art without turning it into decoration. Use the visual language of the urban environment — stencil, spray, wall texture — within the formal constraints of a printed publication.

"I use whatever it takes to communicate a feeling — if it ends up in a gallery, that's a coincidence."
